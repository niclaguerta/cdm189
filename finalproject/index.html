Dunnigan Hills of Zamora, California

Located in the Dunnigan Hills of Zamora, California, Matchbook Winery benefits from a unique microclimate. The nearby hills catch cool delta breezes in the evenings, which help develop grape acidity and enrich color and flavor. This natural cooling, combined with the rocky, well-drained Corning Red Gravel Loam soil, creates ideal growing conditions for full-bodied varietals like cabernet sauvignon and petite sirah.

The vineyard covers 374 acres, divided into varietal plots for small-batch, high-quality wines. An additional 870 acres produce grapes for other wineries, and 300 acres are used for dense olive groves. Matchbook’s commitment to sustainability is evident in its California Sustainability Certification, its plans for organic and regenerative certifications, and eco-friendly practices like sheep grazing, cover crops, and solar panels, which offset nearly two-thirds of its energy use.

Family Owned and Operated

Fifth-generation farmers John and Karl Giguiere founded Matchbook Winery in 2005, drawing on a long family history in Yolo County agriculture. The name “Matchbook” comes from John’s childhood fascination with fire—an interest that led to a few literal sparks and inspired the winery's branding.
