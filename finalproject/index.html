The Green Commitment of Matchbook

At Matchbook Winery, sustainability isn’t just a buzzword; it’s a philosophy woven into every aspect of their operation. As John Giguiere, the owner and founder, succinctly puts it, “It’s the right thing to do.” This ethos has guided Matchbook in becoming a California Sustainable Vineyard, a prestigious certification achieved in 2022. This third-party-audited credential not only reflects their commitment to environmentally and socially responsible farming but also ensures their practices are economically viable—proving that green initiatives can make sense for both the planet and business.

Certifications like this isn’t just about recognition. It’s about market differentiation and consumer trust. According to the California Sustainable Winegrowing Alliance (CSWA), sustainability gives brands a competitive edge, appealing especially to younger consumers who are willing to pay for responsibly produced wine. It’s no surprise then that Matchbook’s efforts, from renewable energy use to regenerative farming techniques, align seamlessly with growing consumer demand.

East of Napa, Left of Center

Matchbook’s vineyards are located in the Dunnigan Hills, a region with soil and climate conditions ideal for growing high-quality wine grapes. The Corning Red Gravel Loam soil, known for its excellent drainage and biology, supports sustainable farming practices. Additionally, the Mediterranean climate and cooling delta breezes help preserve grape acidity and flavor.

These natural advantages demonstrate how sustainability can work alongside environmental factors to enhance wine quality. Matchbook’s focus on these characteristics aligns with broader global efforts, such as New Zealand’s Sustainable Winegrowing program, which similarly leverages regional attributes to create premium wines.

Family Owned and Operated

Fifth-generation farmers John and Karl Giguiere founded Matchbook Winery in 2005, drawing on a long family history in Yolo County agriculture. The name “Matchbook” comes from John’s childhood fascination with fire—an interest that led to a few literal sparks and inspired the winery's branding.

As a family-owned business, Matchbook is committed to preserving the land for future generations. This approach is reflected in their efforts to balance agricultural productivity with environmental stewardship, ensuring that the winery’s operations remain viable and sustainable.

Sustainability in the Vineyards

Matchbook Winery incorporates a range of practices in their vineyard operations to enhance soil health, conserve resources, and promote biodiversity.

Sheep Grazing:
Sheep graze across all of Matchbook’s estate vineyards in both fall and spring. This practice not only controls vegetation but also enriches the soil with carbon through sheep manure, which is crucial for building organic matter. This organic matter forms the foundation of Matchbook’s organic and regenerative farming program, reducing the need for synthetic fertilizers while fostering healthier soils.

Cover Crops:
Cover crops provide shade, reducing summer temperatures and evaporation from bare soils, which helps conserve water. Additionally, cover crops attract arthropods and beneficial insects that break down plant material into organic matter, creating biological pest controls and further reducing reliance on synthetic inputs.

Compost Tea:
To support their soil health initiatives, Matchbook applies an organic compost tea to their vineyards each fall. This mixture includes humic acid chips, organic mined gypsum, chicken manure, and locally sourced almond shells. Together, these components increase organic matter, improve soil structure, and enhance photosynthesis—the process that fuels healthy vine growth and high-quality grape production.

Sustainability in Energy Use

Renewable energy is a key component of Matchbook’s sustainability strategy.

Solar Panels:
Matchbook has installed four acres of solar panels that provide approximately 65% of the energy required for their vineyard and winery operations. This reliance on renewable energy lowers their dependence on conventional energy sources, reduces greenhouse gas emissions, and supports their broader commitment to sustainable practices.

The Role of Certification

The California Sustainable Vineyard certification serves as an important milestone for Matchbook Winery. This certification provides consumers with confidence in the winery’s commitment to responsible practices. It also meets increasing demand for sustainable products, particularly from younger consumers who value transparency in production processes.

Matchbook’s ongoing efforts, including their plans to achieve organic and regenerative certifications, reflect their dedication to continuous improvement. In doing so, they position themselves as a leader in sustainable winemaking, balancing environmental care with high-quality wine production.
